About me

Researcher, educator,
bridge-builder.

I am a second-year PhD scholar at the School of Medicine, University College Dublin, where I study the intersection of AI, technology, and contextual well-being. My research asks a deceptively simple question: can we make well-being explainable — and then teachable — so that AI systems can genuinely support human flourishing rather than merely optimise for proxies of it?

My path to this question is deliberately interdisciplinary. I began as a language educator — teaching Mandarin to hundreds of students across China, Thailand, and Ireland — before turning to research. That pedagogical grounding shapes everything I do: I care deeply about whether knowledge translates into understanding, and whether understanding translates into action.

I have contributed to projects on Long-COVID management, cardiovascular risk in patients with mental health diagnoses, and inclusive digital health with the Irish Heart Foundation and the Europe ACTIVEAID project. My own programme of work centres on the Spectrum of Contextual Well-being (SCW) framework and the ContextWell intervention system.

Beyond research, I am a poet, a 7+ year meditation practitioner, and a multilingual speaker of Chinese, English, and Thai. I believe connecting science, art, and human experience is not a luxury — it is a method.

Research programme

What I'm building.

Spectrum of Contextual Well-being (SCW)

A theoretical framework that conceptualises well-being as a functional capacity — not a static state — shaping perception, judgment, and action across contexts. The foundation of all downstream work.

● Under peer-review

ContextWell Programme

An AI-based well-being intervention and learning prototype that makes the SCW framework interactive. Designed for medical students as a social microcosm, with generalisability to other high-stakes environments.

● Ongoing · Hult Prize national finalist

Well-being Efficacy Framework

A proposed construct extending SCW to capture users' ability to sustain clarity, agency, and effective engagement in complex AI-mediated environments. Bridges learning science and decision science.

● In development

AI Governance & Human Oversight

Examining human oversight not as procedural compliance, but as a dimension of well-being efficacy — reframing AI governance through a human-capacity lens. Published on arXiv; under peer-review at IASEAI 2026.

● Under peer-review

International Healthcare Student Well-being

A systematic integrative review (PROSPERO CRD42024372785) mapping universal barriers and facilitators for international healthcare students' well-being — the population I use as a research microcosm.

● Published · JMIR Res Protoc

Inclusive Digital Health

Collaborative work with the Irish Heart Foundation and the Europe ACTIVEAID project on Long-COVID management, cardiovascular risk in mental health patients, and building a more equitable digital health ecosystem.

● Published · WHO Bulletin

Google Fellowship — Human-Computer Interaction

Bridging the capacity gap
between humans and AI.

As AI systems become more autonomous, a critical structural problem emerges: users increasingly rely on AI-generated outputs while lacking the cognitive scaffolding to critically evaluate or intervene in automated processes. This is the capacity gap — and it is the central problem my Google Fellowship research addresses.

My proposed work extends the Spectrum of Contextual Well-being into an agency-preserving interaction paradigm — designing interactive systems that support users' ability not just to understand their context, but to engage actively with AI-mediated information and decision processes.

This work is grounded in an iterative design-science methodology, with empirical studies in higher education contexts — environments that mirror the complexity of high-stakes AI deployment at scale.
